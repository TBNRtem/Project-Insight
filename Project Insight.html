Bioengineered Algae Reactors in Mongolian Winters

Published: June 21, 2025

This research investigates how algae-based oxygen reactors can reduce air pollution in Mongolia's ger districts. The solution focuses on high-efficiency photosynthetic bioreactors that work in sub-zero temperatures, replacing traditional smoke-heavy heating sources.

Reducing Indoor Air Pollution with Passive Filtration

Published: May 12, 2025

We explored the effect of passive air filters using locally sourced bio-materials like sheep wool and coal ash filters. This low-cost solution is designed to improve indoor air in urban yurts during winter months.

Thermodynamic Efficiency of Mongolian Heating Methods

Published: March 30, 2025

A comparative study on energy loss in traditional coal-burning stoves versus alternative sustainable heating technologies. Results show potential 40% reduction in fuel usage with better insulation and optimized air intake design.
